About Me

All About Me

My name is Derek J. Steele, and I am employed as an auditor by profession.

I am a high school drop-out, later earning a GED.

I served as a full-time representative for The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints in both North and South Dakota, living on/near various Lakota reservations for part of my service.

I graduated from Brigham Young University (Provo campus) in 2010 with a BA in History and a minor in Native American studies, where I also won the 2010 William J. Snow Award in Western and/or Mormon History for my research and thesis on how Native Americans were portrayed in accounts of the Mountain Meadows Massacre.

Currently I am working on a certificate in accounting, as well as a degree in web design and development.

I enjoy serving in the Church, trying new foods, learning about world cultures, drawing portraits and superheroes, watching movies, playing and watching sports, and just getting to know people.
